# EN (Greek)

The Greek word "en" is often translated as "in", but English readers may not always notice that it can carry more than one sense. This article explores how the translation of "en" can affect passages about God, Jesus, and believers living in close relationship.

|                   | [Strong's Concordance](https://biblehub.com/greek/1722.htm) |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Original Word     | ἐν                                                          |
| Part of Speech    | Preposition                                                 |
| Transliteration   | en                                                          |
| Phonetic Spelling | (en)                                                        |
| Definition        | in, on, at, by, with                                        |

## Range of Meaning

Many English Bible readers do not realise that the Greek word "en" can also mean "with", for example:

> After his mother Mary was betrothed to Joseph, before they came together, she was found **with** (ἐν) child of the Holy Spirit. — Matthew 1:18 (NKJV)

> “Behold, the virgin shall be **with** (ἐν) child, and bear a Son, and they shall call His name Immanuel,” — Matthew 1:23 (NKJV)

The same Greek word "en" can also mean "among":

> “But you, Bethlehem, in the land of Judah, are not the least **among** (ἐν) the rulers of Judah” — Matthew 2:6 (NKJV)

> And Jesus went about all Galilee, teaching in their synagogues, preaching the gospel of the kingdom, and healing all kinds of sickness and all kinds of disease **among** (ἐν) the people. — Matthew 4:23 (NKJV)

The same Greek word "en" can also mean "by":

> But I say to you, do not swear at all: neither **by** (ἐν) heaven, for it is God’s throne; nor **by** (ἐν) the earth, for it is His footstool; nor **by** (ἐν) Jerusalem, for it is the city of the great King. — Matthew 5:34-35 (NKJV)

In Matthew 5:34-35, Matthew's audience was not physically inside heaven, earth, or Jerusalem. The context shows that "by" is a better English sense than "in" for that passage. There are [many more examples](https://biblehub.com/greek/en_1722.htm) where the Greek word "en" can mean something other than "in".

## Union With Christ

When Jesus says he is "in" his disciples, the statement does not have to mean that Jesus is physically inside each person. It can describe close communion with him, shared life with the same [family of God](https://eternal.family.net.za/god/family), and representation by [his body](https://kingdom.ofgod.info/christ/body) on earth.

> When used in the sense of “in God,” or “in Christ,” the word “in” refers to a close communion, a tight fellowship. It was part of the covenant language of the day, when people spoke of being either “in” or “cut off from” the covenant.
>
> — Morgridge, pp. 116 and 117; Racovian Catechism, pp. 142 and 143; [Spirit & Truth Fellowship International](https://www.biblicalunitarian.com/verses/john-14-11)

This reading can affect well-known Bible passages such as [John 14-17](https://son.ofgod.info/godhead/jesus-version). If "en" is read as physical location, some teachers understand these passages to mean that believers are inside God and Jesus, while God and Jesus are also inside believers. That view is often used to support the claim that Jesus must be omnipresent like God. Critics may respond that this makes John's language more complex, because Jesus also said that he was going away and would return.

If "en" is read as close fellowship, the same passages can be understood as language about shared love, unity, and covenant relationship. John 14:23, 17:21-23; Matthew 18:20 support this idea because they speak about God, Jesus, and the disciples being united with one another.

## Conclusion

The Greek word "en" has a [range of meaning](#range-of-meaning), so "in" is not always the only possible English translation. In passages about [union with Christ](#union-with-christ), the word can describe close fellowship, shared unity, and covenant relationship rather than physical location.
