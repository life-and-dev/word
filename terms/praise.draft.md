# Praising God

> If you praise God, you express your respect, honour, and thanks to God. -- [Collins Dictionary](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/praise)

> **Great is the LORD**, and greatly to be praised, and His greatness is unsearchable. -- Psalm 145:3 (ESV)

## Praise with words

> Because your steadfast love is better than life, **my lips** will praise you. So I will bless you as long as I live; in Your name I will lift up my hands. -- Psalm 63:3-4 (ESV)

> **My mouth** is filled with your praise, and with Your glory all the day. -- Psalm 71:8 (ESV)

> Let everything that has **breath praise** the LORD! Praise the LORD! -- Psalm 150:6 (ESV)

> Through Him then let us continually offer up a sacrifice of praise to God, that is, **the fruit of lips** that acknowledge His [name](name.md). -- Hebrews 13:15 (ESV)

## Praise with songs

> I will be glad and exult in you; I will **sing praise** to Your name, O Most High. -- Psalm 9:2 (ESV)

> But I have trusted in Your steadfast love; my heart shall rejoice in Your salvation. I will **sing to the LORD**, because he has dealt bountifully with me. -- Psalm 13:5-6 (ESV)

> All the earth worships you and **sings praises** to You; they **sing praises** to Your name.” Selah -- Psalm 66:4 (ESV)

> **Sing to God, sing praises** to His name; **lift up a song to Him** who rides through the deserts; His name is the LORD; exult before him! Father of the fatherless and protector of widows is God in His holy habitation. -- Psalm 68:4-5 (ESV)

> I will give thanks to You, O LORD, among the peoples; I will **sing praises** to You among the nations. -- Psalm 108:3 (ESV)

> **Sing praises** to the LORD, for He has done gloriously; let this be made known in all the earth. -- Isaiah 12:5 (ESV)

> And above all these put on love, which binds everything together in perfect harmony. And let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts, to which indeed you were called in one body. And be thankful. Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly, teaching and admonishing one another in all wisdom, **singing psalms and hymns and spiritual songs**, with thankfulness in your hearts to God. And whatever you do, in word or deed, do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him. -- Colossians 3:14-17 (ESV)

The original "hymns" were found in the Bible and the majority in the book of Psalm. Unfortunately the Israeli did not preserve the original melodies and there are no recording of any notes in the bible.
