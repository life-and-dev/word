# Praising God

Praising God is a central theme throughout the Bible, often described as an expression of respect, honor, and gratitude toward the Creator. It is more than just a musical performance; it involves the heart, the mind, and the voice in various forms. Why does the Bible place such high importance on praise, and how has this practice evolved from ancient shouts of joy to the structured hymns we hear in churches today? This article explores the different ways believers have historically expressed their devotion to God.

> If you praise God, you express your respect, honour, and thanks to God. — [Collins Dictionary](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/praise)

> **Great is the LORD**, and greatly to be praised, and His greatness is unsearchable. — Psalm 145:3 (ESV)

> Let everything that has **breath praise** the LORD! Praise the LORD! — Psalm 150:6 (ESV)

> Through Him then let us continually offer up a sacrifice of praise to God, that is, **the fruit of lips** that acknowledge His [name](name.md). — Hebrews 13:15 (ESV)

(A sacrifice of praise refers to a spiritual offering of thanksgiving instead of animal sacrifices.)

Yes, the world is established; it shall never be moved; **he will judge the peoples** with equity.” Let the heavens **be glad**, and let the earth **rejoice**; — Psalm 96:9-11 (ESV)

## Authenticity of Modern Hymns

Some critics argue that modern hymns are less biblical than the ancient Psalms because they are composed by humans rather than divinely inspired biblical authors. However, proponents of modern hymnody point out that the Bible itself encourages the singing of "new songs" (Psalm 33:3, 96:1) and "spiritual songs" (Colossians 3:16). These compositions allow each generation to express their faith using their own language and cultural musical styles while remaining grounded in biblical truth.

## Conclusion

Praising God is an essential biblical practice expressed through [words](#praise-with-words), [songs](#praise-with-songs), and [shouts](#praise-with-shouts). While the ancient [Psalms](#praise-with-songs) remain a foundational source of worship, the tradition of composing [modern hymns](#praise-with-songs) continues to allow believers to celebrate God's character and deeds across different cultures and eras. Whether through spoken acknowledgment or musical performance, praise remains a vital way for individuals to honor the LORD.
