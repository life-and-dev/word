# The Biblical Meaning of Name

In modern English, a name is primarily a label used to identify an individual or object. The ancient languages of the Bible (Hebrew and Greek) convey a much richer understanding when they use the term. Throughout the Old and New Testaments, "name" often points to something far more profound than a simple designation. Investigating these original meanings can transform how we read familiar passages. It also clarifies the nature of identity and authority in the biblical world.

## The English Definition

In English the word "name" means:

> a word or a combination of words by which a person, place, or thing, a body or class, or any object of thought is designated, called, or known. — [dictionary.com](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/name)

However, in both Hebrew and Greek (the original languages of the Bible), the meaning of name is much more than a designation or identifier. For example, the following verse suggests a meaning deeper than simple identification:

> His eyes are like a flame of fire, and on his head are many diadems, and he has **a name written that no one knows but himself**. — Revelation 19:12 (ESV)

In this context, a name known only to the individual suggests a private character or a status that others cannot fully grasp.

The meaning of the word "name" in [Hebrew שֵׁם ("shem")](https://biblehub.com/hebrew/8034.htm):

* name (to identify), for example Genesis 2:11, 2:19-20, 4:17, 26:33; Ezekiel 24:2
* character, for example Proverbs 21:24
* ownership, for example 2 Samuel 12:28
* reputation, for example Genesis 12:2; 2 Samuel 7:9; Jeremiah 32:20; Nehemiah 9:10
* designation, for example Genesis 4:26

The same word "shem" is also translated as

* "fame" in Zephaniah 3:19
* "renown" in Ezekiel 39:13
* "byword" or "infamous" or "subject of gossip" or "notorious" in Ezekiel 22:5, Ezekiel 23:10

The meaning of the word "name" in [Greek ὄνομα ("onoma")](https://biblehub.com/greek/3686.htm):

(Note: "onoma" is the Greek word for "name", often referring to one's authority or reputation.)

```
Original Word     : ὄνομα, ατος, τό
Part of Speech    : Noun, Neuter
Transliteration   : onoma
Phonetic Spelling : (on'-om-ah)
Definition        : a name, authority, cause
```

* name (to identify), for example Mark 3:16
* authority 
  * "as a rank", for example Matthew 10:41
  * "on behalf of", for example Mark 9:38; Acts 1:15, 10:48
  * "in obedience of", for Matthew 28:19; John 5:43; Acts 8:16
* reputation, for example Matthew 6:9; Luke 1:49

## "Name" as a Magic Word

Some modern translations might be interpreted as suggesting that a name functions as a formula for a request. For example, the New Living Translation (NLT) states:

> At that time you won’t need to ask me for anything. I tell you the truth, you will ask the Father directly, and he will grant your request ***because you use my name***... — John 16:23 (NLT)

However, the biblical concept of "name" does not refer to a magical incantation. A more accurate translation of the underlying thought reads:

> And in that day ye shall ask me nothing. Verily, verily, I say unto you, Whatsoever ye shall ask the Father **in my name**, He will give it you. — John 16:23 (KJV)

## "Name" as an Authority

The term "in the name of ..." means authority. This is evident in the following scriptures:

> And when they had set them in the midst, they inquired, 
> 
> “By what power or **by what name** did you do this?” 
> 
> Then Peter, filled with the Holy Spirit, said to them, 
> 
> "Rulers of the people and elders, if we are being examined today concerning a good deed done to a crippled man, by what means this man has been healed, let it be known to all of you and to all the people of Israel that **by the name of** Jesus Christ of Nazareth, whom you crucified, whom God raised from the dead — by him this man is standing before you well..." 
> 
> — Acts 4:7–10 (ESV)

> Peter said, "...In the name of Jesus Christ of Nazareth, rise up and walk!" — Acts 3:6 (ESV)

It is not Peter's personal power. Peter commanded under the authority of Jesus Christ for the healing to take place. It was God's power that performed the healing. Peter acted as an agent, or representative, on behalf of God.

> But Paul was greatly annoyed, and turned and said to the spirit, "I command you **in the name of** Jesus Christ to come out of her!" — Acts 16:18

In this case Paul commanded a spirit to come out on behalf of God, in His authority.

> And the crowds that went before him and that followed him were shouting, “Hosanna to the Son of David! Blessed is he who comes **in the name of the Lord**! Hosanna in the highest!” — Matthew 21:9 (ESV)

The crowd's reaction was not based on the common Hebrew name "Jesus." Many other Jews shared that name. Instead, the significance lay in the fulfillment of the prophecy in Zechariah 9:9. This prophecy was given by YHVH (the personal name of God, often translated as "the LORD") as seen in Zechariah 9:1. This connection gave Jesus the right to be recognized as a king. The crowds understood that Jesus arrived in the authority of YHVH.

> “Now we command you, brethren, **in the name of** our Lord Jesus Christ, that you keep aloof from every brother who leads an unruly life and not according to the tradition which you received from us.” — 2 Thessalonians 3:6 (ESV)

Similar themes appear in the Old Testament. Moses did not struggle to identify God, as he already knew the name YHVH. Rather, Moses faced the challenge of returning to Egypt as an exile without legal standing or authority. God addressed this by sending Moses with His "name," signifying divine authority to deliver a message.

> Then Moses said to God, “Indeed, when I come to the children of Israel and say to them, ‘The God of your fathers has sent me to you,’ and they say to me, **‘What is His name?’** what shall I say to them?”<br/>
> And God said to Moses, “I AM WHO I AM.” <br/>
> And He said, “Thus you shall say to the children of Israel, I AM has **sent** me to you.’ ”<br/>
> Moreover God said to Moses, “Thus you shall say to the children of Israel: ‘The LORD (YHVH) God of your fathers, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob, has **sent** me to you. This is My **name** forever, and this is My memorial to all generations.’ Go and gather the elders of Israel together, and say to them, ‘The LORD God of your fathers, the God of Abraham, of Isaac, and of Jacob, appeared to me, saying...” — Exodus 3:13-16 (NKJV)

Isaiah's prophecy does not suggest a literal change of personal names. Instead, it indicates that God gives a command or authority to gather those who are spiritually ignorant:

> **Everyone who is called by My name**,<br/>
> Whom I have created for My glory;<br/>
> I have formed him, yes, I have made him.<br/>
> Bring out the blind people who have eyes, and the deaf who have ears.
> 
> — Isaiah 43:7-8 (NKJV)

## Commandment

> You shall not take **the name** of the LORD your God in vain, for the LORD will not hold him guiltless who takes **his name** in vain. — Exodus 20:7 (ESV)

According to the [Interlinear Bible](https://biblehub.com/interlinear/exodus/20-7.htm), the direct translation is:

> Yahweh will hold [him] guiltless not for in (vain / nothing / worthless / falsehood) your God of Yahweh the (identity / reputation / authority). You shall take not in (vain / nothing / worthless / falsehood) His (identity / reputation / authority) who

In other words:

This implies that an individual is guilty before the LORD (Yahweh) if they treat His authority as worthless or insignificant. Misrepresenting His identity is a violation of this command. One is accountable if they do not submit to His authority, as doing so creates a false representation of who He is.

## Jesus submission

Jesus prayed opened his prayer with...

> "Our Father in heaven, hallowed be **your name**..." — Matthew 6:9 (ESV)

According to the [Interlinear Bible](https://biblehub.com/interlinear/matthew/6-9.htm), the Greek for that verse reads:

> Ἁγιασθήτω τὸ ὄνομά σου 

which directly translates to

> hallowed the name You

Which could be understood as

"Set apart for a special purpose the identity, reputation and authority of You"

or

"I acknowledge that You have an identity and authority which is not like any other"

Opening a prayer in this manner indicates that subsequent requests are submitted to God's authority. This acknowledges His unique identity. Rather than directing God, the person praying submits to His authority, as seen in the petition "your will be done" — Matthew 6:10 (ESV).

## "Name" as Honor

The word "name" can also represent "glory" or "honor." Jesus explained this concept when addressing His authority:

> I do not receive glory from people. But I know that you do not have the love of God within you. I have come in my Father's name, and you do not receive me. If another comes in his own name, you will receive him. How can you believe, when you receive glory from one another and do not seek the glory that comes from the only God? — John 5:41-44 (ESV)

## Conclusion

The biblical concept of a [name](#the-biblical-meaning-of-name) extends far beyond a simple identifier. It encompasses an individual's [authority](#name-as-an-authority), [reputation, and character](#the-english-definition). Scripture warns against treating this [authority as worthless](#commandment) and clarifies that using a name is not a [magic formula](#name-as-a-magic-word). Instead, it represents a deep [submission to divine will](#jesus-submission) and can even be synonymous with [honor and glory](#name-as-honor).
