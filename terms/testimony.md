# Testimony

Is faith truly a private matter? While many today view religious belief as a strictly personal affair, the ancient scriptures describe faith as something that naturally overflows into public life. This article explores the biblical concept of testimony (the act of sharing one's personal faith and experiences with God) and the impact of openly acknowledging one's convictions in a world that often demands silence.

## Acknowledging the Son

The scriptures emphasize that a relationship with God is inseparable from one's public stance regarding Jesus Christ. Jesus Himself stated the importance of this acknowledgment:

> Everyone who acknowledges me before men, I also will acknowledge before my Father who is in heaven, but whoever denies me before men, I also will deny before my Father who is in heaven. — Matthew 10:32-33 (ESV)

The apostle John further reinforces this connection between internal belief and external confession (publicly declaring one's faith):

> No one who denies the Son has the Father. Whoever confesses the Son has the Father also. — 1 John 2:23 (ESV)

> We have seen and **testify** that the Father has sent his Son to be the Savior of the world. Whoever confesses that Jesus is the Son of God, God abides in him, and he in God. — 1 John 4:14-15 (ESV)

## The Cost of Witness

Publicly declaring one's faith is not always easy. Even in modern times, some [believers face opposition when they publicly testify Jesus is Lord](https://youtu.be/VRsBsitt6rQ). This reality highlights that testimony is often a sacrificial act of courage.

## Common Objections to Public Faith

Some argue that faith should remain private to avoid offending others or causing social division. They might suggest that a "personal relationship with God" means a "hidden relationship." However, the biblical perspective suggests that true transformation cannot be concealed. [Just as light naturally shines in a dark room](https://kingdom.ofgod.info/light), a life changed by God naturally becomes visible to others.

Critiques often point to "performative" or "hypocritical" public displays of religion as a reason to keep faith private. While it is true that public testimony should be genuine and humble, the existence of counterfeits does not invalidate the necessity of the real thing. A sincere testimony is not about self-promotion but about pointing others toward the source of one's hope.

## Conclusion

Biblical testimony is the natural result of a life transformed by a personal relationship with God. The scriptures teach that [acknowledging the Son](#acknowledging-the-son) is essential to a relationship with the Father. This internal faith is [validated through public confession](#confession-and-salvation), even when faced with the [potential cost of witness](#the-cost-of-witness). Ultimately, while faith is deeply personal, it is never intended to be private, as it serves as a beacon of hope to the world despite [common objections](#common-objections-to-public-faith).
