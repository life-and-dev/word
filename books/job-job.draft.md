# Reliability

Jewish tradition has long recognised the wisdom and value of Job, incorporating it into their religious teachings as well the Christians included it in their biblical canon for centuries.

[The author of is unknown.](https://www.thomasnelsonbibles.com/blog/who-wrote-the-book-of-job) Some speculate that the author could be:
* Job - [he could have recorded his own story from firsthand experience](https://thecrosstalk.com/knowledgebase/old-testament/wisdom-poetry/who-wrote-book-job)
* Elihu - [his speech is recorded in a lot of detail and this led to believe some that he could have been the author himself](https://insight.org/resources/bible/the-wisdom-books/job)
* Moses - [due to the book's setting in the patriarchal period](https://thecrosstalk.com/knowledgebase/old-testament/wisdom-poetry/who-wrote-book-job)
* Solomon - [because Solomon was known to collect wisdom literature](https://www.bibleref.com/Job/survey-of-Job.html)
* An unknown group of editors - [because the complex structure the theological themes suggest potentially skilled multiple contributors](https://thecrosstalk.com/knowledgebase/old-testament/wisdom-poetry/who-wrote-book-job)

No, bible author directly quoted any scripture from the book of Job. However, both Ezekiel 14:14-20 and James 5:11 refers to Job as a real person and that his story was general knowledge for their audiences.

## Manuscript Evidence

[Fragments of the Book of Job have been found in Cave 4 and 11 near Qumran](https://www.logos.com/product/36375/the-dead-sea-scrolls-of-job-from-cave-4-and-cave-11) as [part of the Dead Sea Scrolls](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Job). This proofs that the ancient transmission of the text of Job was in circulation among the Jewish communities who were living in that region during the Second Temple period.
